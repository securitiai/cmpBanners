The Andromeda Galaxy

Our cosmic neighbor, Andromeda, is a spiral galaxy approximately 2.5 million light-years from Earth. It's the closest major galaxy to the Milky Way and is destined to collide with our own galaxy in about 4.5 billion years. This galactic merger will reshape both galaxies.

Black Holes

Black holes are regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They are formed from the remnants of massive stars and play a crucial role in the evolution of galaxies.
